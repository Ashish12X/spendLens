# GTM.md — Go-to-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

Not "startups." The specific user is:

**Title:** Engineering Manager or CTO  
**Company stage:** Series A or post-revenue bootstrapped, 8–40 people  
**Current pain:** They received the monthly AI tools invoice, noticed it was higher than expected, and have no structured way to evaluate whether the spend is justified. They're not actively looking for a solution — they don't know this problem has a name.  
**Secondary user:** Solo founder or technical co-founder at pre-seed/seed who personally pays for Cursor, Claude, and ChatGPT and wonders if there's overlap.

The EM at a 25-person startup is the sweet spot. They have enough spend to feel the pain ($500–2000/month across the team), authority to act on recommendations, and credibility to share the audit with a CFO or board.

## Getting the First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1:**
1. **Show HN on Hacker News** — title: "Show HN: Free tool to audit your AI tool spend (found $380/mo on my own stack)." Personal story + screenshot of the audit. HN Show HN front page = 3–10k visits on a slow day.
2. **Post in Indie Hackers** — "I built SpendLens to audit my own AI tools and found I was paying for 3 team plans as a solo founder. Here's what I found." Personal narrative, link to audit.

**Week 2:**
3. **Targeted Twitter thread** — "I audited 50 AI tool stacks. Here's what I found:" followed by anonymized data from real audits. Link to tool at the end. Tag relevant accounts like @swyx, @levelsio.
4. **Cold DM EMs on LinkedIn** — 20 personalized messages to Engineering Managers at Series A companies: "Built a free tool that checks if your AI tool stack is over-provisioned. Here's what a 25-person team typically looks like. Want to try it?"

**Week 3:**
5. **Post in r/ExperiencedDevs** — "How much does your team spend on AI tools? I built a free audit tool and ran it on our stack." Authentic post, not promotional spam.
6. **Reach out to 5 startup newsletter writers** — Software Lead Weekly, TLDR Tech, Programming Digest. Email: "Built a free audit tool your readers who run engineering teams might find useful. 2 minutes, no signup. Happy to share anonymized aggregate data from early users."

**Week 4:**
7. **Follow up with users who shared their audit** — reach out to anyone who shared via the public URL, ask for a tweet or LinkedIn post in exchange for early access to benchmarking data.
8. **Post aggregate findings** — "We've seen 50+ AI spend audits. Here's what the data says about team-size vs spend correlation." This is the viral content that drives ongoing traffic.

## Unfair Distribution Channel

**Credex's existing relationship with AI vendors.** Credex already has deal flow with companies that overforecast AI usage (that's the core business). This means Credex has direct relationships with buyers and can identify companies that just signed large AI contracts and are now rationalizing their tooling. A Credex sales rep who closes a $30k ChatGPT Enterprise deal can hand the customer SpendLens as a value-add audit tool — making SpendLens a retention tool, not just acquisition. No other company in this space has this distribution shortcut.

## Week-1 Traction — What Success Looks Like

If the HN post hits front page:
- 500–3,000 audits completed in 7 days
- 50–150 email leads captured (assuming 5–10% lead conversion from audit)
- 3–8 organic tweets from EMs and founders
- 2–5 Credex consultation requests from high-savings cases

Minimum "working" signal:
- 100 audits completed
- 10 email leads captured
- 1 inbound consultation request

If we get fewer than 100 audits from HN + IH + Reddit combined, the copy or distribution channel needs rethinking — the product works, the framing doesn't.
